SEAHORSE


Nearly all scientific inquiry is based on the paradigm of hypothesis testing in which the association between pre-identified experimental factors and final states of a system are explored. However, this approach is limited by the state of our current understanding of the systems we wish to study.

SEAHORSE (Serendipity Engine Assaying Heterogeneous Omics Sampling Experiments) was created to allow users to develop new hypotheses from large population-based multi-omic datasets. Using both phenotypic and genomic variables in both the Genotype-Tissue Experiment (GTEx) and The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) we pre-computed all pairwise associations between phenotypic variables, between genomic variables in each tissue, and between phenotypic and expression in each tissue.

SEAHORSE provides not only the opportunity for users to search for new and unexpected hypotheses that can be tested in future experiments or other studies, but also creates opportunities for findings from other studies to be confirmed in GTEx and TCGA.
